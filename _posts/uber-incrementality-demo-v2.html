The real Uber story: Sundar Swaminathan, Head of Brand & Growth Marketing Data Science at Uber, ran a 3-month incrementality test on Meta ads across the US and Canada. Result: zero incremental value. Uber had saturated major urban markets — most riders would have signed up organically anyway. Savings: $30–35M annually. This project builds the measurement system that catches that finding.
Five modules — one pipeline
1Impression deduplication
Quantifies the impression overcounting problem using HyperLogLog. Same user counted 3.65× — power analysis N is wrong by 265%.
HyperLogLogCriteo dataset
2Power analysis advisory
Advises on test viability without pre-experiment inputs. Three proxy methods. Calibrated to real Meta/Google CPM ($8–10).
Monte CarloBootstrap
3Conversion lift simulator
Mirrors Meta/Google user-level holdout tests. Proves naive Z-test is biased due to cohort time structure.
Cohort-corrected IVWDiD
4DMA geo holdout
Single-channel geo experiment with DiD, synthetic control, and CausalImpact. Replicates the Uber $35M near-zero Meta finding.

5Multi-cell cross-platform experiment
4-cell factorial: Meta only / Google only / Meta+Google / Holdout. Measures cross-platform synergy across 210 US DMAs. Negative interaction (-3.02×) reveals audience cannibalisation.

Academic grounding
2015Lewis & Rao — "The Unfavorable Economics of Measuring the Returns to Advertising." Quarterly Journal of Economics. Median CI on ROI over 100pp wide. Informative tests require 10M+ person-weeks.
2017Kerman, Wang & Vaver (Google) — "Estimating Ad Effectiveness using Geo Experiments in a Time-Based Regression Framework." Basis for TBR geo holdout methodology.
2007Flajolet et al. — "HyperLogLog: the analysis of a near-optimal cardinality estimation algorithm." Powers the impression deduplication engine (±0.8% error, 16KB RAM).
2012Bakshy, Eckles & Bernstein — "Uncertainty in Online Experiments with Dependent Data." Motivation for cohort-corrected standard errors in Module 3.
